Index Credibility FAQ

What is the Index Credibility and what are we trying to do?

We built this tool because we're tired of all the fake news and biased reporting out there. Our app helps you figure out which news articles you can actually trust.

We don't tell you what to think - we just give you the facts about where the news is coming from and how reliable it seems to be. Then you can decide for yourself what to believe.

How does the Index Credibility show the full picture?

One of the key features of our tool is showing you similar news articles from different sources. This helps you:

  • See how different news outlets cover the same story
  • Compare different perspectives on the same event
  • Spot when important facts might be missing from one source
  • Understand how different media organizations frame the news
  • Get a more complete picture of what's really happening

This way, you're not just getting one side of the story - you're seeing how multiple reliable sources are reporting on the same event.

What is the "Credibility Index"?

The Credibility Index is a score we give to news articles based on how trustworthy they seem. We look at:

  • How accurate the facts seem to be
  • Whether the article seems biased or fair
  • How reliable the news source usually is
  • If the article gives you enough background information
  • How current the information is

We combine all these factors to give each article a score from 0-100. Higher scores mean more trustworthy news.

How does the Index Credibility help me?

Our tool helps you in several ways:

  • You can spot articles that might be biased or missing important facts
  • You can see which news sources are generally more reliable
  • You can compare how different news outlets cover the same story using our similar articles feature
  • You can learn to think more critically about what you read
  • You can avoid sharing fake news with your friends and family

Basically, it gives you a quick way to check if that article you're reading is probably trustworthy or if you should be skeptical.

How do you ensure your scores are fair?

We work hard to keep our analysis fair:

  • We train our AI on diverse news sources, not just one side
  • We're transparent about how we calculate our scores
  • We regularly check our system for any biases
  • We have real journalists review tricky cases
  • We listen to user feedback about our analysis
  • We show you articles from multiple sources so you can compare different viewpoints

We also show you exactly how we came up with each score, so you can decide for yourself if you think we're being fair.

What's your vision for the future?

We'd love to see a world where:

  • News organizations are transparent about who owns them and who funds them
  • Everyone learns how to think critically about what they read online
  • Social media shows you why you're seeing certain posts, not just what they want you to see
  • Good journalism gets the support it deserves
  • You can easily find different viewpoints on any topic
  • Technology helps us understand things better, not just manipulate us

It's not about agreeing with everything you read - it's about having the tools to decide what to believe. And seeing multiple perspectives helps with that!

How does the "same topic articles" feature work?

Our "similar articles" feature works by:

  1. When you analyze an article, our system identifies the key topics and facts
  2. At the same time it searches for other articles covering the same topic from different reliable sources
  3. We show you these articles ranked by how relevant they are and how trustworthy their sources are
  4. You can compare how different news outlets are reporting on the same event
  5. This helps you see the full picture, not just one side of the story

It's like getting a second (and third, and fourth) opinion on the news you're reading!
